The writer primarily presents the atmosphere as isolated yet full of a tempestuous presence. This is conveyed when the library is first described as being ancient and how "the space felt preserved, carefully held in place, as though change was something that reluctantly happened". By the library being preserved it plants an idea of it being preserved for someone or something to happen which creates unease and discomfort. This foreshadowing technique causes us as readers to understand that the protagonist is unsafe. The atmospherical discomfort is further enhanced by the old touch to the library as ancient desolated environments are linked with deaths and unsettling thought further re amplifying the uncertain events in the future.

Further more the library is presented as a labyrinth which established an unsafe atmosphere and the consequences of an effect on sanity. This is first introduced when we learn that "it was easy to imagine loosing your sense of direction, wandering utilitarian the reason you came no longer mattered". This hyperbole presents the psychological impact this library can cause on a person foreshowing what could possibly happen to the protagonist in the future. The library is show as being unpredictable and dangerous towards whoever enters.
