The writer describes the atmosphere of the library as an old magical place, almost like a labyrinth.

The writer uses a metaphor to compare the library to a labyrinth, "the shelves stretched deeper" and "losing sense of direction", "wandering... no longer mattered". These suggest the idea of a confusing place that is hard to navigate, like a maze, however the fact you are able to "wander" until nothing "mattered" gives connotations of a magical place.  

This idea of magic and old tales is continued in the description of the books. "Faded", "cracked spines" and "dust". These suggest an old piece preserved in time, while creating a feel of a magical, never-ending bookshelf, like one in a fairytale.
