The writer creates a tense, quiet almost watchful atmosphere in the library when he writes, "dust drifted through the pale light and settled slowly". The alliteration 'dust drifted' suggests that the library is old and has not been cleaned very often. The lexical phrase 'pale light' implies that the library is not taken care of as much as it should be as the light is nor very bright. It also creates tension as the pale light creates quietness and loneliness. The verb 'settled' suggests that the dust is staying there on the shelf where it falls, its not gojng to bemoved in a long time. This has an affects on the reader as they may think of the library as not very clean and quiet.

Another way the writes uses language is a sense of imagery. "The smell of paper and ink lingered, dry and faintly bitter". The writer writes like this to create an image in the readers mind of how the library smelt like. Further on in the text, on the next line it talks about how the library looked. The writer uses the senses that a person has to create a picture for someone who is reading this text and to show that the library is dark and tense.
