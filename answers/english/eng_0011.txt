The writer uses language to describe the atmosphere as archiac and ancient. This is shown when the writer uses personification to describes the encyclopaedia with “cracked spines.” This suggests that the books in the library are old and rotten and have been used for a long time. Alternatively this could suggest although the books have seen better days they may be valuable therefore why they have been kept in the library. The fact that they have still not chosen to throw them away shows that they still have a lot of knowledge to give to people and they are useful. Furthermore the writer uses language to present the atmosphere in the library as cluttered and special. Demonstrated in “Old magazines were stacked in uneven piles.” The adjective “uneven” suggests that they are not organised and there is no specific order for the magazines and books in the library and that they form “long aisles” for visitors to get lost among and trapped in. This could be a metaphor of how the visitors may get lost in the fiction once they’re within the walls of the library. The writers purpose here maybe is to highlight how on a literal level the library is messy, old and untidy but on a metaphorical level a book worm would be in a sense of heaven.
