The writer uses verbs such as drifted, settled and collecting to describe the dust in the building, these verbs make the reader feel as though there is a presence lingering in this library however may be subtle or almost invisible. This creates a sense of mystery or slight fear in the reader or a hint of anticipation.

The use of commas from "the space felt preserved" slows down the readers thoughts and makes us slow down to experience the anticipatory feeling that Nia is feeling.

The writer uses the personification stretched to describe the bookshelves and uses imagery throughout this paragraph to create a clear picture in the readers head of what this library looks like. The verb stretched  helps us invisage how vastly long this library is, and how it cannot be filled with just normal bookshelves, they have to be stretched in order to fill all the crevices of this building. This can create a sense of unease in the reader, especially using the phrase "losing your sense of direction", we as readers are worried for Nia in this untouched eerie environment however we want her to explore this building since we anticipate what she may find around another corner.
