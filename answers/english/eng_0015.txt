The writer uses a range of language to describe the atmosphere of the library as musty but tranquil place.

 The use of sensory language shows how the library was depicted as an antique somewhere that had historical meaning that had significance and had to be kept in good conditions. " The smell of paper ink lingered dry and faintly bitter" The adjective 'bitter' creates a sense that the smell is long lasting and make create a pungent taste in you mouth. The use of imagery also suggest how the library has been holding its goods for along time " their faded photographs still visible at the edges". The adjective faded suggest the period of which they have been sitting there also creating an unsettling feeling for us as readers a it makes you wonder when was the last time someone came to the library.
