The writer creates a quiet, mysterious atmosphere by using imagery, descriptive language, and sentence structure. The phrase “dust drifted through the pale light” suggests stillness and age, as “drifted” implies slow movement and calm. The light is described as “pale”, which creates a subdued and slightly eerie mood rather than warmth.

The writer also personifies the library to make it feel alive and controlling. The silence is described as “heavy” and as though it has “weight”, which suggests it presses down on Nia and makes her feel watched. This makes the atmosphere feel tense rather than peaceful.

Descriptive detail is used to make the space feel endless and disorientating. The shelves “stretched deeper into the building” and the aisles “curved out of sight”, suggesting that the library is larger than expected and easy to get lost in. This reflects Nia’s uncertainty and creates a sense of unease.

Sentence structure also contributes to the atmosphere. Longer, flowing sentences slow the pace, mirroring the quiet and stillness of the library. Overall, the language makes the library feel calm but unsettling, as though it demands silence and attention.
