The writer uses lpowerful vivid imagery to create a quiet, preserved, and slightly unsettling atmosphere in the library.
The phrase “Dust drifted through the pale light” immediately suggests stillness and age. The verb “drifted” implies slow movement, reinforcing how calm and undisturbed the space is, while “pale light” creates a weak, muted image rather than brightness. This makes the library feel subdued and almost frozen in time.

The writer continues this atmosphere by describing dust settling “slowly” and collecting in “the grooves of the floorboards”, which suggests long-term neglect or at least very little human disturbance. However the writer then contrasts this with “It was everywhere, yet it did not suggest neglect”, showing that the dust is part of preservation rather than decay. This makes the library feel intentionally untouched rather than abandoned.
