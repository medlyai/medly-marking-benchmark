The author meticulously orchestrates the text to present the library to have an eerie yet comforting atmosphere. By describing the appearance and the subtle action of dust which had "drifted", the writer draws the reader's attention to the the intricacies of the library which did not escape the sight of the narrator through the use of alliteration, inculcating into the reader of how the sight of the disarranged yet cared for library struck a cord in the narrator and made them feel at ease. Furthermore, through  the idea that the library itself is portrayed to have been preserved "as though change was something that happened reluctantly", the writer enforces the oddly soothing atmosphere that the library possessed. Through the use of the simile that likens the preserved nature of  the seemingly abandoned library to reluctant change despite the omnipresence of dust makes the library seem like an entity who cares for itself and is cared for, undeniably bringing to the forefront the magnificent comfort the library harbours despite it's discouraging appearance.
The writer delineates the underlying beauty and the enchanted atmosphere of the library, making it the epitome of a bibliophile's heaven. Through the metaphoric use of the phrase "Encyclopedias with cracked spine" the writer emphasises on the previously active library which served as a knowledge hub, as it has served the multitude with knowledge till it was visibly worn out, yet it was perfectly intact, making it seem like a bibliophile's paradise as knowledge was precious and eternal, highlighting the enchanting atmosphere the library held.
