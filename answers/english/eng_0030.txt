The writer uses alliteration in the sentence opener "Dust drifted" which can emphasise the calmness of the building. "Drifted" is a very calm verb which suggests that the building has a relaxed atmosphere and anyone who enters will be immediately calmed from the chaos of the outside world. Another alliterative phase is "settled slowly", which describes how the dust settles and relaxes. The adverb "slowly" conveys the lack of force and pressure that is acting against the dust, which again adds to the relaxed and calm atmosphere in the library.
The short sentence "she breathed in" slows the pace of the story as the reader needs to stop after the sentence to read on. this can affect the reader by calming them as they read, as an affect of the slow pace. This makes them feel like they are in the peaceful, slow paced and calm atmosphere of the library, engulfing them into the story.
