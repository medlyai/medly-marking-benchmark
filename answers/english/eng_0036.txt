Throughout the text, the writer uses careful structural features to grasp our attention and pay closely to the details rather than the facts, to sort of bring us into Nia's shoes. The writer first starts off with a short mundane sentence, however by saying Nia paused, this causes the reader to pause with her, and feel the details in the long listed paragraph that the writer shows in the next paragraph. The writer first moves our focus to the outside, and presents a scene that us as the reader would have usually ignored on our ways to school or work. Instead she first starts explaining each single detail and slowly moves to the door handle. This slight shift in focus to the inside of the room keeps our interest, as she breaks this flow of thought with another short sentence. The writer then tickles our every sense in each paragraph and sends our focus into each corner and nook of the room, allowing us to slowly build a carefully curated picture of the library. At the middle of the text, the writer brings us to the centre of the room, where usually the middle of the text is the climax, however by introducing the desk, we can infer that this table will bring us to the climax of the story foreshadowing more to come from this table. Now the writer shifts our focus to Nia's thoughts without changing the second person narrative, and then jumps from her thoughts to the surroundings once again at the end and brings a cyclical structure, from the beginning where Nia paused, at the end she paused again, and began a new story. Therefore interesting us throughout the whole text as a reader
