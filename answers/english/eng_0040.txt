The use of the short paragraph at the start builds up tension and immediately hooks the reader. It gives an eerie feeling as the pace has gone from long dragged out sentences to this quick, short and powerful paragraph. The tension rises as the reader gets anxious for Nia.
 In the middle of the extract, the constant shift of focus to the different parts of the library create quite an erratic and anxious atmosphere, like something is about to happen, this makes the reader start to expect something is about to happen, thus creating tension and expectancy. 
At the end, the pace slows, only speeding up when she hears a sound, this quick change makes us anxious and wonder what wis about to pop out from this massive library, the tension slowly settles at the when it links us back with a circular narrative. Nothing happens and the cycle repeats for another. This relaxes the reader as now they know nothing is going to happen.
