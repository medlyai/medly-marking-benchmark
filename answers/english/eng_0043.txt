The writer opens the novel with a short, isolated sentence that grounds the reader into the setting whilst also creating an enigma of what the main character's intent is underneath the arch way. The focus then shifts to an unsettling description of the street Nia finds herself in, zooming in on the metal door handle where she rested her hand and let the 'cold bite' into her skin. The constant reflections of the cold weather outside align grabs the reader's attention as it plays with the common association of the cold to be foreshadowing an unsettling event - capturing the readers' attentions and making them want to read on. After Mia enters the library, the focus finally shifts onto the dust that 'preserved' the nature of the library. This contrasts the unease experienced by Nia in the opening paragraphs, as the writer zooms in on this to make the library provide some comfort for Nia, due to change happening reluctantly there - which reassures her with the idea of stability and reduced change.
