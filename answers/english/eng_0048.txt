The writer shifts from third person to second person to directly adress the reader and make them feel the effects of the library. At the beginning the writer uses vivid description to paint a picture of the outside world with many details, then changes to a more calm and slow description of the library, which contrasts the library and separates it from the outside world. The writer uses personification of the library to make it seem like the library is alive and 'watching' Nia. The writer doesn'y explicitly state what Nia is going to do which creates interest, and uses rhetorical questions to create suspense.
