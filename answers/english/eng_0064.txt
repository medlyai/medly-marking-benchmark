I agree that from the moment Nia reaches the table, the writer creates a sense of unease and watchfulness, which gradually shifts as she gains confidence. At first, Nia is cautious and alert, shown in the description: “Her fingers tightened against the edge of the table as she listened, heart beating heavily in her chest.” The detail of her tightening fingers and racing heart conveys her nervousness and makes the reader share her tension, emphasising the library’s watchful atmosphere.

The writer reinforces this sense of observation through personification: “The library felt watchful now, not hostile, but aware.” The library is described almost like a sentient presence, which heightens the unease but also suggests that it is not threatening. This allows the reader to sense both tension and a subtle reassurance, reflecting Nia’s growing awareness of her surroundings.

By the end of the extract, Nia’s confidence emerges. The final line, “Around her, the library remained silent, holding its order and its patience, as it had done for years, while another story quietly began,” suggests that she has embraced the space and her purpose. The calm, enduring quality of the library contrasts with her initial tension, highlighting her development from hesitation to taking action. Through these carefully chosen words and the structural shift from tense description to calm reflection, the writer effectively conveys Nia’s journey from unease to confidence, making her progress both believable and engaging for the reader.
