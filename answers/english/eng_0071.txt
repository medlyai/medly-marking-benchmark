I agree that the writer created a sense of unease in the source. This is because as Nia sets down her backpack she is clearly trembling, however  it is unclear weather this is of excitement or fear. So once 'the contact steadied her' she was able to continue. This suggests that something is happening inside her head that is preventing her from doing what she clearly loves. Writing. Furthermore, the personification of the library seeming to 'expect' her to do something gives the libary a more demanding tone- like an old school headmaster. To the reader this could make her seemed force which creates a sense of unease within the extract.
In spite of this, i disagree with the statement: 'Despite her initial hesitation, by the end it seems she has found the courage to begin something new'. Anyone when writing or reading must first get lost in the book  before they can truly enjoy consuming the media. In the source it is unclear weather Nia is exited to write or being forced, however because 'she wondered' and 'lingered longer than she expected it would suggest that she is in control of the situation and is not being forced into creating literature. I therefore, similarly believe that she is not unconfident when she first begins writing she is just not yet focused on the task at hand. Although she is writing 'cautiously' she is not being forced. She is crafting foundations that she can then comfortably write and write on top of explaining why she gains confidence as she finishes writing.
In summary i do find the initial tone of the extract to be uneasy due to the personification of the library and the feeling that Nia is being watched. However at no point do i think she is unconfident in her writing abilities as she only first has to create the bed that the rest of the narrative will flow on top of.
