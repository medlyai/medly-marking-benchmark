I agree mostly with the statement, although I would say that even from the beginning she had a strong determination to do what she set out to do. When she "set her backpack down and rested her hand on the table", she carries a sense of calmness despite her hesitance. It seems as if she is drawn to the beauty of the "surface... worn down by years of use." The use of this imagery may suggest that perhaps she is hesitant out of admiration of respect for the library's powefful atmosphere, rather than unease or fear. In this atmosphere, "her thoughts felt sharper and more insistent". The use of these adjectives emphasise her determination and eagerness, with nothing to oppress it. The short sentence, "a creak sounded..." creates a sudden spike of tension, which does contribute to a feeling of unease and uncertainty. Through Nia's actions, "froze", "fingers tightened", "listened", "heart beating", the writer implies that she is now fearful. However, this is a temporary feeling as soon "the tension eased". The writer reinforces the idea that the library's power is "not hostile", which returns determination to Nia. We see her confidence grow through the sequence of actions, "sat down, uncapped her pen, and began to write", increasing from "cautiously" to "with confidence", suggesting that at this point her courage is building, she is determined to build something new, and the non-hostile library watches silently, patiently, and immortally.
