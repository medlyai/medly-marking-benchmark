I fully agree with the statement that "from the moment Nia reaches the table, the writer creates a sense of unease and watchfulness. Despite her initial hesitation, by the end, it seems she has found the courage to begin something new." 

Before Nia arrives towards the table, the writer presents Nia as fearful and a sense of unaccomplishment. As Nia approaches the table, the writer shifts her feeling from unease to hopefulness, as "the contact steadied her" This suggests that Nia is not in her fragile state and that she sees more hope and prosperity. The writer foreshadows Nia's growing confidence through the text, as the writer slowly hints the state of clarity as "her thoughts felt sharper and more insistent". Furthermore, the writer uses the line  "space seemed to expect something of her," which contrasts with earlier in the text where the "reason she had come no longer mattered" This proves that Nia's initial view on the library shifts towards a more positive and hopeful aspect. Moreover, the writer states how the library "registered her presence without interest," hinting that it may help her find what she was looking for. Near the end of the text, the writer portrays the library as an element that gives Nia confidence and courage as she "began to write, cautiously at first, then with more confidence". Furthermore, the writer presents the library as full of the power to hold "its order and its patience", which shows how the library became the fundamental reason and importance of growing Nia's confidence once entering the library.
