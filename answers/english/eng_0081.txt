I mostly agree that in this part of the text, Nia goes from feeling unsure to starting something new. At first, she seems very cautious. For example, “she set her backpack down and rested her hand on the table,” and the description of the table as “smooth, worn down by years of use” shows how she’s looking for something to steady her. Even small things matter to her in this space, which makes her nervousness feel very real.

Her unease becomes even clearer when “a creak sounded from somewhere among the shelves,” and she “froze… fingers tightened against the edge of the table as she listened, heart beating heavily.” You can really picture her being alert, almost tense, which shows how aware she is of her surroundings. The library itself is described as “watchful… as though it registered her presence without interest,” which mirrors her own cautiousness—she’s careful and unsure at first.

But then Nia starts to gain confidence. She thinks about why people come to the library and asks herself, “Would this place give her what she was looking for?” Then she acts: “She closed her eyes briefly, then reached into her bag and took out a notebook. The pages were clean and bright beneath the lamp.” The clean pages feel full of possibilities, and it’s a turning point because she starts writing. At first, it’s “cautious,” but then she writes with “more confidence,” which shows she’s starting to feel braver.

The ending line, “while another story quietly began,” makes it clear that something new is starting for her. The writer uses details like her thoughts, her actions, and the quiet library to show how she moves from being hesitant to feeling ready to begin. Overall, the passage shows Nia’s change in feelings really well, from uncertainty to the courage to start something new.
