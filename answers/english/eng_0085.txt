I largely agree that, from the moment Nia reaches the table, the writer creates a strong sense of unease and watchfulness, yet also shows that she gradually finds the courage to begin something new. The scene feels tense at first, but it becomes increasingly purposeful as Nia settles into the space.
At the start of this section, Nia is still tentative and uncertain. When she “rested her hand on the table” and the contact “steadied her,” it suggests she needs reassurance before she can continue. The verb “steadied” implies that she feels unbalanced or unsure, reinforcing the idea of unease. The writer also emphasises the oppressive silence: “There was nothing here to distract her.” This absence is described almost as a pressure, intensifying her inner tension. The statement that the space “seemed to expect something of her” is particularly unsettling, because it personifies the library as if it is silently judging her. This creates the impression of a watchful environment, making both Nia and the reader feel slightly uncomfortable.
The sense of unease reaches its height when “A creak sounded from somewhere among the shelves.” The uncertainty of “somewhere” means the source cannot be seen, and the sound breaks the stillness abruptly. Her reaction — “She froze” — shows instinctive fear, reinforcing how tense she still is. The phrasing “heart beating heavily” suggests physical anxiety, and the way she listens for the sound again shows she feels scrutinised. Even when the noise stops, she remains “alert,” which implies that the watchfulness has not disappeared entirely. The description of the library as “aware… without interest” is chilling, as if she is noticed but not necessarily welcomed. This contributes to a haunting, watchful atmosphere.
However, despite this unease, the writer also shows Nia gradually gaining confidence. After the tension passes, she begins to reflect more deeply. She wonders what others come here for — “answers… reassurance… time enough to think” — which shows she is starting to engage with the purpose of the library rather than just its strangeness. The rhetorical listing suggests she is searching for her own reason to be there, signalling emotional growth. Importantly, she still questions, “Would this place give her what she was looking for?” — a sign that uncertainty remains — but the fact she is now considering possibilities instead of hesitating indicates movement towards courage.
The decisive moment comes when she “reached into her bag and took out a notebook.” This deliberate action contrasts with earlier moments of hesitation. The description of the pages as “clean and bright” symbolises opportunity and new beginnings. The shift in verbs — from “hesitated” and “froze” to “sat down, uncapped her pen, and began to write” — shows a clear change in pace and confidence. Although she writes “cautiously at first,” the phrase “then with more confidence” confirms that the environment ultimately empowers her rather than paralyses her. The final line — “another story quietly began” — reinforces the idea that this moment marks the start of something meaningful.
Therefore, I strongly agree with the statement. The writer skilfully balances tension and watchfulness with growing determination. Through shifts in tone, physical reactions, and symbolic detail, the extract shows Nia moving from anxiety to action. Even though the unease never disappears entirely, it becomes part of what motivates her, making her choice to begin writing feel brave and significant.
