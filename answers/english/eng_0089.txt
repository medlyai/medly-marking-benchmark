I mostly agree that from the moment Nia reaches the table, the writer creates a sense of unease and watchfulness, which gradually shifts into a quiet confidence by the end of the extract. The writer carefully balances Nia’s internal uncertainty with subtle reassurance from the environment, allowing her change in mindset to feel natural rather than sudden.

At first, the sense of unease is strongly conveyed through Nia’s physical reactions and heightened awareness of her surroundings. When she sets her backpack down and rests her hand on the table, the writer notes that “the contact steadied her,” implying that before this moment she felt unsettled and in need of reassurance. This suggests that Nia is not fully comfortable in the space yet and needs something tangible to ground herself. The stillness of the library intensifies this feeling, as it leaves her “thoughts… sharper and more insistent,” indicating that the silence forces her to confront her uncertainty rather than escape it. The writer reinforces the sense of watchfulness by personifying the space, as “the space seemed to expect something of her,” which creates the impression that Nia is being silently observed or judged. This heightens the tension, as the reader shares her sense of pressure and anticipation.

The unease deepens further when “a creak sounded from somewhere among the shelves.” Structurally, this moment interrupts the calm description and introduces a brief spike in tension. Nia’s reaction — freezing and gripping the edge of the table — reflects her vulnerability and suggests that she is still on edge. Although the sound does not return, the library now feels “watchful,” which maintains an atmosphere of unease even after the immediate threat has passed. The fact that the building is described as “not hostile, but aware” is particularly effective, as it implies quiet scrutiny rather than danger, making the atmosphere unsettling in a subtle, psychological way.

However, despite this tension, there is a gradual shift towards confidence as Nia begins to engage with her purpose. The writer signals this change through Nia’s reflections, as she starts to consider what others “came here hoping to find.” This moment of introspection suggests that her focus is moving away from fear and towards understanding. The rhetorical questions about “answers” and “reassurance” reveal that Nia is beginning to articulate her own needs, which marks a turning point in her emotional state.

By the end of the extract, Nia’s decision to take out her notebook and begin writing clearly demonstrates her growing confidence. The pages are described as “clean and bright,” which contrasts with the dust and age of the library, symbolising possibility and a fresh start. The adverbs “cautiously at first, then with more confidence” directly track her emotional progression, confirming that while her uncertainty has not completely disappeared, she has gained the courage to act despite it. This makes her development believable and understated.

The final line, in which “another story quietly began,” reinforces this sense of calm resolution. The library’s continued silence now feels supportive rather than threatening, suggesting that Nia has found a sense of belonging within the space. Overall, the writer effectively conveys Nia’s transition from unease to quiet determination, making the reader agree that although the watchfulness never fully disappears, it ultimately becomes a source of focus rather than fear.
