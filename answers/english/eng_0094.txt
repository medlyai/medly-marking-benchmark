Calmly the captains two feet stay firmly planted in the boat as tempestuous gushes of water crash on the oak floor. The little sun that's left is trying to settle the ocean but instead being swallowed by its ferocity. Once the anchor had been planted in the sea bed, the malevolent winds tempted the boats stability, wanting it to be rid of all human presence. A tide so manipulative hid under the waves singing a tune of serenity hoping to trick a sailor into jumping in and meeting their fate.  

Cold, bitter, merciless air held hints of salt with underlays of regret. Storms like this find enjoyment in challenging those who dare to set sail on their sea ; their final goal is always the same, destruction.

No body comes out alive. 

Gloomy clouds which dispersed in the sky are ready to thunder down the pellets of rain they had stored up since their last battle. The captains once steady feet have become unstable sending him flying across the boat. 

In the distance land lays with rows of people lining up on the shore observing the captains fate. Over there is safety, securing. Out of reach from the claws of the ocean. In the distance other sailors begin their journey back to secure grounding knowing that this fight with water is not one that can be one.
