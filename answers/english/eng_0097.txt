Warning sirens sounded from the lighthouse standing on the cliffs telling everyone to prepare. The water was eerily still, reflecting the darkening sky like a black mirror, waiting.

Crews on boats were scrambling to prepare as the salt stung their eyes and filled their noses, sharp and biting. Panic spread across the water: some dropped their rusted anchors wherever they could. Others raced towards shore. Pulling down sails as the wind grew stronger howling and wailing like a banshee. 

The sky was turning grey and black clouds loomed in the distance threatening to bring heavy rain down upon the sea. 

The wind gradually built up speed twisting and turning in every direction. Icy cold. Drops of rain began to pummel the surface of the ocean like cascading bullets being fired from above relentlessly colliding with the sea.

There were sharp rugged cliffs surrounded by oceans. They had sharp points like knives jutting out at all angles. Towering waves from the dark sea stood up tall like a horse rearing on its back legs charging towards the rocks. 

The two collided as though they were at battle. The waves were sliced by the cliffs, sending icy cold spray in every direction.

As the storm drew nearer the sky drew darker until almost no light could be seen. The ocean was like an angered monster tossing boats around haphazardly and sending gigantic waves tumbling towards the shore. The storm built up speed until the sky was a black sheet of clouds resisting all attempts made by the sun to break through. The thunder rolled across the cliffs like distant cannons. 

The earth became silent. The siren was quiet; crews had hurried inside and boarded up windows; sails had been pulled down and were no longer beating against the wind. The storm was here.
