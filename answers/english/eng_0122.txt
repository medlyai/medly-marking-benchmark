In Source A the orchard is presented as a calm, peaceful and reflective place with an almost magical atmosphere. The writer focuses on sensory and emotional experiences, by describing how "the orchard became the whole of my world, complete and self-contained" and how "time seemed to move differently, stretching and slowing". The purpose of the writer seems for visiting the orchard seems to be personal - to find comfort, notice beauty and reflect. The writer presents that he has a close, emotional relationship with the orchard, returning each year and still feeling the same sense of joy, reassurance and gratitude. 

In contrast, Source B presents the orchard in a practical, work-faced way. The atmosphere is instructional and chronological, emphasising careful planning and attention. The writer explains the steps for creating a productive orchard such as evaluating the soil, finding the right trees and managing pests. Here the purpose is for productive labour and achieving a successful harvest, rather than having an emotional and affectionate relationship with the orchard as highlighted in Source A. The writer shows the joy of having a fruitful harvest when he says "when the harvest is gathered and proves wholesome and abundant, the keeper finds satisfaction". This conveys the writer's work-related connection with the orchard showing no personal affection towards the orchard itself but only the joy in a healthy and successful harvest.

Overall, the two sources differ in purpose and atmosphere—Source A is calm, reflective, and emotional, while Source B is structured, practical, and focused on work. They also show contrasting relationships with the orchard: one is intimate and personal, the other is disciplined and professional.
