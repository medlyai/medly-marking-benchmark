In source A the writer describes the atmosphere as calming and paradise like. The apples are likened to a "piece of the sun" indicating the writer's feeling of being surrounded by great and luminous beauty. She also states that time "trickled like honey" in the orchard, implying that it acted as a sanctuary for her and a place to escape the chaos of life. Overall the writer indicates that she goes to the orchard to experience a sense of "joy" and feel secure in the natural haven.
In source B the writer has a far less romantic and more professional relationship with the orchard describing it as a "productive orchard". He talks about "commitment" and "vigilance" highlighting how, for him, the orchard is less of a sanctuary and more of an arduous chore. However, the writer makes clear that  there is still beauty in the orchard as he describes the "satisfaction" in his labour, as well as stating that the "rewards are considerable" showing his belief that his effort and attentiveness was fruitful and his appreciation for the outcome.
