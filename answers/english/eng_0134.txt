In source A, the writer clearly thinks of the orchard as a safe and peaceful place to relax and collect their thoughts. The darkness did 'not feel threatening' and the narrator felt an 'unexpected joy'. This shows the sentimental love that they feel for the orchard. Furthermore, in their 'years since, that evening has stayed with' them. This phrase further emphasises the personal connection that the narrator felt with that orchard.
However in source B, rather than a personal connection felt by the narrator, it is an economic connection. Phrases such as 'costly errors' and 'limits food production' express that the narrator only thinks about their economic opportunities when relating to the orchard. Furthermore, 'best results' and 'reduces yield' also objectifies the orchard and only considers material gain rather than a living system.
Both source A and B explore very different opinions felt towards the orchard. In source A it is a more sentimental feeling and in source B the orchard is seen as an economic opportunity. However, both sources end by linking the orchard to 'attention' and 'patience'. In source A, this 'patience' and 'attention' is through observing the orchard and in source B this 'patience' and 'attention' is through 'steady work and thoughtful planning' in relation to maximising economic benefits.
