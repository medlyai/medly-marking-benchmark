The writers in these two extracts have very different relationships with the orchards they are talking about. In source A it is all about the beauty and stillness of the orchard and how it made her reflect on memories of the past year, whereas source B is about the practicalities of using an orchard to produce crop that can be sold. The author from source A uses descriptive phrases like " golden-red" , " air felt charged" and "orchard became my whole world" to convay this sense of harmony with the orchard. However in source B the author uses phrases of practicality such as "productive orchard" , " land...properly assessed" and " prevent costly errors" creating the tone of someone who is only doing this for money but is doing it with care because of that not from love to the orchard.
