THE WRITER USES LANGAUGE TO DESCRIBE THE ORCHARD AS BEAUTIFUL AND ENTRANCING AT DUSK. HE DESCRIBES THE 'DEEP GOLDEN RED' APPLES AS CARRYING A 'PIECE OF THE SUN WITHIN THEM'. THE APPLES BEING FILLED WITH SUN SUGGESTS THAT THEY ARE WARM AND WELCOMING AND TASTE FRESH AND PERFECT ASWELL AS LOOKING PERFECT WITH THEIR 'DEEP RED' COLOUR. THE SUNLIGHT AFFECTS THE WHOLE ORCHARD POSITIVELY. THE GRASS 'SHIMMERED' IN THE 'LAST AUTUMN LIGHT'. THE GRASS SHIMMERING MAKES THE ORCHARD SEEM ALMOST MAGICAL AND ENJOYING THE LAST REMENANTS OF SUN AND WARMTH. THIS IS EMPHASISED BY THE AUTHOR DESCRIBING THE ORCHARD AS 'ALIVE' WITH THE 'DAY SLIPPING AWAY' SUGGESTING THAT THE DAY 'SLIPPING AWAY' IS UNWANTED AND WHAT GIVES LIFE AND JOY TO THE ORCHARD AND ALL WITHIN IT. THE ORCHARD TAKES THE WHOLE ATTENTION OF THE AUTHOR AS IT BECAME HIS 'WHOLE WORLD, COMPLETED AND SELFCONTAINED'. THIS SHOWS HOW THE BEAUTY OF THE ORCHARD AT DUSK IS ALL CONSUMING AND IS ALL THAT THE AUTHOR NEEDS AND WANTS AT THAT VERY MOMENT.
