At dusk, the orchard is described as a warm and calm place, even though it is dark. 'The branches overhead arched like silent guardians'. The simile 'like silent guardians' portrays the extent of how safe the narrator feels within the presence of the orchard, even during nighttime. The adjective 'silent' further evokes the tranquil and peaceful setting. 'Eventually, I turned to leave'. The word, 'eventually' illustrates that the narrator stayed at the orchard for a while, even after dusk. This suggests their close relationship and safety felt between themselves and the orchards warm company.
When the narrator is walking back 'through the rows', they 'felt a quiet promise forming, though' they 'could not have put it into words'. The personification 'felt a quiet promise forming' presents the close relationship between the narrator and the orchard. The adjective quiet, displays its calm atmosphere and the noun 'promise' emphasises the close and warm relationship between the orchard and the narrator.
The writer describes the orchard as a warm and loving place, even though it is after dusk. There is clearly a strong relationship felt by the narrator and the orchard, even though it had been engulfed by darkness, showing how powerful the narrator feels this connection actually is.
