In source A, the writer presents the orchard at dusk as being almost like a paradise, and a haven of peace. The writer describes that at the orchard, "time seemed to move differently, stretching and slowing, like honey dripping from a spoon." The fact that time feels different in the orchard to in any other place reflects how the atmosphere felt in the orchard is unique and unable to be found anywhere else. It elevates this orchard into being almost like heaven because it is sometimes said that there is no sense of time in heaven. The comparison between the orchard and heaven emphasise how at home the writer feels here. The simile "like honey dripping from a spoon" once again reflects on the slow paced nature of this orchard. Also, the use of "honey" may suggest the long standing nature of this orchard. Honey is widely known to last indefinitely when stored correctly. Therefore, the orchard could be compared to honey. As long as it is taken care of properly, the beauty of this orchard will stay forever. Although many things will change, some things will stay the same and this is what the orchard represents. This haven of peace will not expire, giving the writer a place where they can always go to. Also, the writer describes that  "the orchard seemed to breathe along with [them], steady and unhurried." The personification here gives the orchard the human quality of breath. It elevates the orchard to be aligned with the writer. The use of personification bridges the gap between the human world and the non human world. The orchard is in tune with the writer to highlight the connection between them. Also the orchard is descibed to be "steady and unhurried". These adjectives reinforce the idea that the orchard is a place where time slows down and where the writer is able to forget about the rushed nature of our daily lives. Ultimately, the orchard is a magical place for the writer, representing maybe even what heaven would look like for the writer.
