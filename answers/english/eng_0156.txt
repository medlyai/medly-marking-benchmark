The writer uses emotive language to enhance the welcoming and safe nature of the Orchard and its awareness. "The approaching darkness did not feel threatening." The verb "approaching" suggest the inevitability of darkness, which usually creates an image of danger and risk, however the Orchard protects the voice from it. "the branches overhead arched like silent guardians." The metaphor shows the safety the voice feels while in the orchard. This helps the reader understand the importance of the Orchard to the writer.

The writer also uses language to show the positive effect dusk in the Orchard has on the awareness of the voice. "A reminder to notice what remained." The noun "reminder" describes how the voice feels the Orchard is purposeful and connected to them. The phrase "notice what remained" implies resilience and helps the voice become more self-aware.

As well as this, the writer uses language to describe the Orchard at dusk as sacred and rare. "I watched, transfixed, aware that the display would last only minutes." The adverb "transfixed" implies the awe the beauty of the Orchard gives to people. The noun "display" links the Orchard to a piece of art, reflecting its perfectness.
