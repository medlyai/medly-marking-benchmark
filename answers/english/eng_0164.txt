The writer presents the orchards as an ethereal place that evokes feelings of nostalgia while reminiscing  on the first time they beheld the orchard. By describing how each fruit glowed a "deep golden red", the writer encapsulates the beauty that radiates from the fruits glow, painting a vivid picture in the reader's head. Furthermore, the metaphoric use of the verb "glowed" positions the fruits as jewels, showing how dear and indispensable they are to the author, and even perhaps they exceed the precious minerals themselves, contributing to the concept of the enchanting beauty of the orchard


The orchard is portrayed to have flourishing trees that  bore fruit which's taste transcended beyond any other fruit. The writers memory of the" sharp and vivid" taste of the fruit which tasted different to any other fruit they had ever tried. The adjective "sharp" used t describe the taste also presents the fruit's distinguishable taste as a knife which slashed through the writer's existing memory of the processed fruits they had tried from the super markets, making the reader curious to try it as a simple taste was enough for the writer to disregard the universal taste of fruits obtained from supermarkets.
