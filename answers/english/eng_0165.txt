The writer uses vivid imagery and figurative language to create a calm, reflective atmosphere at dusk. Visual imagery is used to emphasise the beauty of the scene, such as when the apples are described as glowing “as though they carried a small piece of the sun within,” which makes the orchard feel warm and almost magical. The sunset colours are carefully layered, with “crimson, burnt orange and lavender,” suggesting richness and gradual change as day fades into night.

Personification is also used to bring the orchard to life. The trees are described as “thoughtful old friends,” which creates a sense of familiarity and comfort, while the orchard itself seems to “breathe,” suggesting harmony between the writer and nature. This reinforces the peaceful mood.

The writer also appeals to the senses. Touch is used when the bark is described as “rough and cold,” grounding the writer in the moment, while taste is highlighted when the apple is “sharp and vivid,” making the experience feel real and immediate. Similes such as time stretching “like honey dripping from a spoon” slow the pace of the writing, mirroring the stillness of dusk.

Overall, the language creates a tranquil, reflective atmosphere that shows dusk as a moment of quiet beauty rather than darkness or loss.
