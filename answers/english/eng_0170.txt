The writer uses language to describe the orchard at dusk as comforting. This can be shown in the quote: "stooped like thoughtful old friends". The simile used here, likening the trees to old friends personifies them, giving them the warm, embracing qualities of friends. The adjective "old" gives them a sense of authority and bestows a level of trust, further enhancing these qualities of comfort. This makes the reader ease into the story, using the trees to create an atmosphere of cosiness. The verb "stooped" does not only signify physical curvature, but carries humility, as if the trees are bowing down in respect. The tone therefore becomes soft and communal, the trees' support is unwavering, creating an intimate aspect towards them. The apples are also described to have "carried a small piece of sun within it". This provides a mystical element to the apples, and by extension the trees, by using celestial imagery to highlight the brightness they emit, not only physically, but metaphorically. The apples are sweet, nourishing and beautiful, highlighting the virtuous nature of the orchard. Later, the branches of the trees "arched like silent guardians". This further instils the protective nature and companionship of the trees which the writer feels towards them. The simile used here gives them the qualities of warriors, glorifying them and evincing their impact on the writer. Therefore, language is used effectively to describe the orchard at dusk as comforting.
