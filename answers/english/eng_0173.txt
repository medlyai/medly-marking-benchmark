At dusk, the orchard is presented as a magical place to experience. For example, the imagery of the fruit that "glowed a deep golden-red" from the sun, which makes them feel supernatural and magic. This magic power of the orchid seems to connect with the reader as they "rested [their] hand against" the "rough and cold" trunk. Connotations of the hand is significant as it is often used for connecting with people, and holding hands, whereas now it is trying to unite with the magic from the trees. The imagery of "rough and cold" would ordinarily seem unpleasant, but seem to feel peaceful and refreshing to the writer.
There is also a further sense of peace within the orchard, as the "quiet closed in". The metaphor of the quiet could be unsettling, however their is a calming air between the trees from the autumn light that is comforting and relaxing. This therefore is reinforced by the "darkness [not feeling] threatening", as it seems safer in the orchard - which also juxtaposes and subverts expectations of danger of being along and in the dark, for the reader.
