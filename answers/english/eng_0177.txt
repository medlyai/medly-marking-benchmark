The writer starts of with a simile: " The trees stood in neat rows, stooped like thoughtful old friends." Here, the writer is trying to convey how the trees have a welcoming presence rather than threating using personification to give them human-like characteristics to convey how pleasing the appearance of the trees in a line feels to the writer using the noun "friends" to show the familiarity with the trees and suggesting that the orchard has it's own purpose. 

In the second paragraph, the writer relies heavily on sensory imagery to immerse the reader. The grass “shimmered,” a verb associated with light and movement, which reinforces the idea that dusk is fleeting and delicate.

The writer then continues by saying: "It was as though the orchard itself were waiting," using personification again to describe the feelings he had to the reader of the orchard around them. The writer felt as though there were a person waiting for them to  create an effect of anticipation within the reader as they slowly walked into the orchard. 

In the fourth paragraph, colour imagery dominates the description of dusk. The sky “began to change,” suggesting a gradual transformation rather than a sudden shift. Words such as “crimson,” “burnt orange,” and “pale lavender” create a layered visual effect, showing the complexity and beauty of the sunset. Additionally, the orchard is described as seeming to “breathe,” a clear use of personification that links the natural world to the writer’s own calm breathing, reinforcing harmony between person and place.

In the fifth paragraph, the writer introduces contrast in emotional language. The “sudden, unexpected joy” contrasts with earlier references to routine and responsibility, highlighting how dusk in the orchard disrupts everyday worries. The apple’s taste is described as “sharp and vivid,” adjectives that suggest intensity and authenticity. This contrasts with “supermarket” apples, implying that the orchard offers something more real and honest. The branches arching like “silent guardians” is another example of personification, presenting the orchard as protective as darkness approaches.

In the sixth paragraph, the writer uses metaphorical language to show the orchard closing at dusk. The orchard is said to be “beginning to close itself to the day,” suggesting it has control over time and light. This reinforces the idea of dusk as a natural boundary. The phrase “quiet promise” is deliberately vague, using abstract language to suggest an emotional commitment rather than a spoken one, showing how deeply the moment affects the writer.
