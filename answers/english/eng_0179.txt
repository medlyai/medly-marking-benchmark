The writer uses language to describe the orchard at dusk as memorable and angelic. The writer does this by using personification such as the "branches overhead arched like silent gaurdians" as if the place is some heaven that is being protected by the nature there. The author couldve described them as anything else but chose guardian to show that its an angelic place almost magical worth protecting, even objects and things try to protect it at all costs, making it seem valuable to people wether is valuable in money or valuable in the peace it brings to people.
The sentence "that evening has stayed with me. I carry its warmth like a small flame" shows us the reader still remembers the orchard at dusk, it's stayed with him ever since. The simile suggests that the place warmed their heart as flames are associated with fir and warmth, almost like they have been burned by a small flame and they will never forget the impacts of that small flame. The writer using a simile as intense as a flame emphasises the impact this orchard had on them after dusk.
