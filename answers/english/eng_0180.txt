Source A describes the dusk as a moment of central transition for the text. The writes uses the arrival of dusk to transition from her busy 'blur' of the day and reach a state of stillness. As the dusk arrives the trees become 'silent guardians' that reassure the girl that darkness is not scary as it is all just part of nature. Instead of her being petrified of the encapsulating darkness the orchard transforms into a safe haven and refuge for her to ensure she is cared for. 'Silent' does not highlight the timidness and lack of support but the permanent  and stable protection the orchards provide. As well the 'heaviness' of the branches filled with apples further support that the abundance of care and support the orchard provides by being a refuge really encapsulates the trees omnibenevolent nature towards not only the girl during darkness but towards the apples to grow and protect them from predators. Altogether the dusk does not just heighten the girls apprehension but also increases the protection given by the orchards.
As the sky transitions for sunset to dusk we see the ephemeral colors of the sky 'surrender' to dusk. Although we could see this as something forceful of nature symbolizing that time will always move on, a deeper meaning could symbolize how this is a normal procedure that the beauty must do gracefully as it is only temporary. This change shows that even though the setting has now changed and darkness has engulfed her surroundings, she still carries a 'small flame' with her which further insinuates the peace and permanent serenity she experiences with the orchards. Altogether this highlights dusk is something that must occur and should not be something we fear so we can see the hidden protectors and ones who care for us most.
