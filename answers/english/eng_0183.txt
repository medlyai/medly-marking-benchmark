BOTH AUTHORS CONVEY THE IDEA THAT THE ORCHARDS PROVIDE POSITIVE QUALITIES OR FEELINGS TO PEOPLE. THE AUTHOR OF A USES THE METAPHOR OF 'JOY DOES NOT ALWAYS ANNOUNCE ITSELF LOUDLY' TO SUGGEST THAT EVEN MINISSULE SEEMINGLY ORDINARY THINGS SUCH AS ORCHARDS CAN FILL SOMEONE UP POSITIVELY THROUGH VIEWING ITS BEAUTY. ON THE OTHER HAND THE AUTHOR OF B STILL USES A METAPHOR 'IT (THE ORCHARD) TEACHES ATTENTION, RESTRAINT AND RESPECT' BUT SHOW THAT THROUGH CARING AND MAINTAINING THE HEALTH OF THE ORCHARD THE KEEPER IMPROVES AS A PERSON AND LEARNS HOW TO BECOME BETTER TOWARDS OTHERS AND NATURE.

BOTH AUTHORS ALSO EXPLORE THE WAY THAT THE ORCHARD GRABS AND KEEPS HOLD OF SOMEONES ATTENTION FROM THE VIEW OF AN ONLOOKER VERSES THE VIEW OF AN ORCHARD KEEPER. THE AUTHOR OF SOURCE A WRITES THE METAPHOR ' THE ORCHARD BECAME THE WHOLE OF MY WORLD' TO EMPHASISE HOW THE BEAUTY OF THE ORCHARD HAS CAUSES IT TO BE HIS SOLE FOCUS AND ALL HE CAN THINK ABOUT AND ALL THAT HE NEEDS TO SURVIVE. THIS CONTRASTS TO HOW THE AUTHOR OF SOURCE B WRITES 'THE ONGOING CARE OF AN ORCHARD REQUIRES STEADY COMMITMENT'. THIS SHOWS THE VIEW OF A KEEPER OF AN ORCHARD AND HOW THEY MUST BE CONSTANTLY THINKING AND CARING FOT THE ORCHARD BACAUSE OTHERWISE IT COULD DIE OR PRODUCE BAD FRUIT.
