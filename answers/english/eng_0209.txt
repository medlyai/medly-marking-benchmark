Both sources are about the writers connection with orchards. In source A the writer feel's a connection with the orchard because of how alive and yet still it seemed. For example in the quote "i watched transfixed" shows her awe for the beautiful evening and the word "unhurried" proves how at ease she feels in this space. In contrast source B has a connection with the orchard in order to produce a good crop and prove her delicacy with it. When she mentions "the keeper finds satisfaction" it reminds us that even though her article was about practicalities she still cares for her orchard with as much love as anyone could. The very last word in source B is " care" enforcing the fact that you can care for something even if your purpose for doing so is connected to business.
